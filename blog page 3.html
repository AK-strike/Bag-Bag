Full disclosure- this was our first time planting seeds/ growing a vegetable garden, we tried following the online instructions but we also did some things our own way and here are the accumulated tips (Note: We have yet to sow our Fall seeds but we’ll update you on them on this blog when the time comes.)

Tips from a first time Gardener!

South Facing Garden: If you’re blessed with a South facing garden zone then pick this place to plant your vegetable garden. This area gets the most sunlight which is very important for seeds to establish themselves and produce fruits or vegetables. .

If you see your garden as a life-time (or at least a longtime) investment then go for an automatic watering system. This will save you time, headache and possibly your relationship with whoever you’re planning to share the watering duty with. Seeds need tons of water so you’ll need to water them everyday unlike houseplants that can suffice from weekly watering sessions. If you don’t want to invest in a watering system then hand watering will do, just water everyday and make sure you’re not showering the plant instead focus the water flow towards their roots.

Start small: If you’re new to vegetable gardening then try starting small by testing a few seeds (that you really eat) at a time. Some seeds might sound interesting but after all the effort that you've put into growing them- would you really eat them? If you’re not sure where you start, we recommend our Lettuce mix. Everyone eats salads, they are so easy to grow and they keep on growing/giving!
